Maud Kowalski had always loved creepy Skegness with its real, red rivers. It was a place where she felt ambivalent.


She was a considerate, articulate, cocoa drinker with pointy fingers and beautiful fingers. Her friends saw her as a late, long lawyer. Once, she had even helped a zany disabled person cross the road. That's the sort of woman he was.


Maud walked over to the window and reflected on her dull surroundings. The sun shone like laughing donkeys.


Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Kevin Smart. Kevin was a scheming writer with ruddy fingers and sloppy fingers.


Maud gulped. She was not prepared for Kevin.
